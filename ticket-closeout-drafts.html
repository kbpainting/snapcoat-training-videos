#53

Can't add primers to rates

Greg Chung · greg@elevatepaintpros.com · bug → completed

Message
Fixed — primers, stains and any other materials now show up in the paint dropdown no matter which category you're building a rate for. The cause: that dropdown was only offering products typed as "Interior" on interior categories (and Exterior on exterior ones), so anything typed as Primer, Stain or Other had nowhere to appear. Those aren't interior- or exterior-specific, so they now show everywhere. Where to find it: Settings → Production Rates → open any rate → Default Paint Product. One tip: if you relabelled any primers as "Interior Paint" to work around this, you can switch them back to Primer under Settings → Paint Products and they'll still appear in every category. No need to rebuild anything.
#54

Duplicate rates

Greg Chung · feature → completed

Message
Added — there's a Copy button on every rate row now, next to Edit and Delete. It clones the whole rate: coat rates, coverage, substrate width, default paint product and its settings. Where to find it: Settings → Production Rates → expand a category → the copy icon on any rate row. Worth knowing: the copy arrives named "… (Copy)", and that label is the one your customers see on proposals. So we drop you straight into the editor right after copying — rename it, adjust your numbers, save. That way a "(Copy)" can't accidentally end up in front of a customer.

#32

"Not assigned to a company" signup lockout

Zach Bellin · bug → completed (fixed back in March, never closed out)

Message
Resolved — and apologies this one sat open longer than it should have. The cause: our signup flow wasn't checking whether an email already existed before creating an account. Signing up again with slightly different capitalisation produced a second account that wasn't attached to your company, which is why you could log in but couldn't do anything, and why adding yourself came back as "user already exists". We removed the duplicate, attached your account properly, and added a case-insensitive email check so it can't happen to anyone else. Nothing needed on your end — you're all set. If anything like it ever crops up again, just reply here and we'll get on it the same day.
